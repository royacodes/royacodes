## Claude Opus 4.6: The "Long-Run Operator"

Anthropic has positioned Opus 4.6 as something different: a dedicated execution engine. It is currently the strongest model for multi-step coding, deep debugging, and long-running tasks.

Three features make this shift possible:

- **Agent Teams in Claude Code:** It doesn't just write a snippet; it manages the repository.
- **1M-Token Context (Beta) & Context Compaction:** It can hold massive amounts of information without "forgetting" the beginning of the project.
- **Larger Max Output:** It can finally deliver massive artifacts (full codebases, long reports) in a single go without getting cut off.

> Claude is positioning itself as the worker that stays late to finish the job.

## OpenAI: The "Manager of the Fleet"

While Anthropic is building the ultimate worker, OpenAI is building the ultimate headquarters.

Along with their new flagship coding model (**GPT-5.3-Codex**), OpenAI has rolled out **Frontier**. This isn't just a chat interface; it is a platform to deploy and manage "AI Coworkers."

Think of Frontier as an HR department for your agents. It handles:

- **Onboarding:** Getting the AI up to speed on your company context.
- **Scoped Permissions:** Controlling what the AI can and cannot touch.
- **Performance Review Loops:** Treating agents like employees inside enterprise stacks.

## The Verdict

If you are building a real company or optimizing a serious workflow, the takeaway is simple:

**Claude is becoming your execution engine.**
**OpenAI is building the management layer for your fleet of agents.**
